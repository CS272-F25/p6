History of Hats

A brief look at why this site exists and how to explore everything it offers.

Bearskin Cap

Worn by Ötzi the Iceman, this bearskin hat is one of the oldest known examples of headwear. It was designed purely for survival in harsh alpine climates, featuring a chin strap made of leather.

  • Origin: Ötztal Alps (Austria/Italy border)
  • Era: c. 3300 BCE (Copper Age)
  • Source: South Tyrol Museum of Archaeology
Pschent (Double Crown)

The Double Crown of Ancient Egypt. It combined the Red Crown of Lower Egypt and the White Crown of Upper Egypt to symbolize the pharaoh's power over the unified lands.

    • Origin: Ancient Egypt
    • Era: First Dynasty (c. 3100 BCE)
    • Source: Historical Reconstruction
The Lappvattnet

Based on textile fragments found in Sweden, this represents some of the earliest woven fabric in the Nordic region, showing that prehistoric headwear was both functional and crafted with skill.

  • Origin: Västerbotten, Sweden
  • Era: c. 6000 BCE (Mesolithic)
  • Source: Västerbottens Museum
Kente Kufi

A brimless, short, and rounded cap worn by men in many parts of Africa. This specific version features Kente cloth patterns, originating from the Ashanti Kingdom of Ghana, symbolizing royalty and wealth.

  • Origin: Ashanti Kingdom (Modern-day Ghana)
  • Era: 17th Century (Traditional)
  • Source: Traditional Ashanti Design
1920s Baseball Cap

Before the structured caps of today, baseball players wore soft wool caps with shorter brims. This 1920s Spalding style marks the transition from amateur headgear to the modern professional standard.

  • Origin: United States
  • Era: 1920s (Golden Age of Baseball)
  • Source: Keyman Collectibles / MLB Collectors
Cloche Hat

The iconic "bell-shaped" hat of the 1920s Flapper era. Designed to be worn low on the forehead, it was the perfect companion to the short bob haircuts that defined the decade's rebellion against Victorian norms.

  • Origin: Paris, France
  • Era: Early 20th Century (Pop. 1920s)
  • Source: Library of Congress (George Grantham Bain Collection)
